How many planets are needed for a stellium in Vedic astrology?

In Vedic astrology, the standard for a significant planetary conglomeration or stellium is typically four or more planets occupying a single sign (rƒÅ≈õi) or house (bhƒÅva). What is the most powerful house for a stellium in a Vedic chart?

The most powerful and impactful houses for a stellium are the Kendra houses‚Äîthe 1st (self), 4th (home/happiness), 7th (partnerships), and 10th (career/karma). These angular houses represent pillars of life, and a stellium there concentrates immense energy into foundational areas, making those themes dominant and often publicly significant. Among these, the 10th house stellium is considered particularly potent for shaping one's destiny, career, and public role, as it is the house of action and dharma. However, a stellium in a Trikona house (1st, 5th, 9th) can bring great fortune (lakshmi) and spiritual support to that area, as these are houses of luck and higher wisdom.

In Vedic astrology, the dispositor lord‚Äîthe planet that rules the sign where the stellium is placed‚Äîis the single most important factor in determining the results of the stellium. Can a stellium in Vedic astrology be a bad thing?

A stellium is not inherently good or bad; it is a concentration of karma. However, it can certainly manifest as a major challenge if the stellium is afflicted. What is the difference between a conjunction and a stellium in Vedic terms?

In Vedic terminology, all planets in a stellium are in conjunction with each other, but a conjunction (yuti) typically refers to the pairing or grouping of two or three planets. A stellium (graha sam≈´ha) specifically denotes a larger, more impactful cluster of four or more planets. The distinction is one of degree and emphasis. Furthermore, Vedic analysis of any conjunction, including within a stellium, meticulously applies rules of Graha Yuddha (planetary war) when planets are extremely close, which can designate a victor and a defeated planet, adding another layer of interpretation not commonly used in Western astrology.
